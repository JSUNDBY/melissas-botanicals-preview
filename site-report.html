Observations

What we found

Each finding below has the technical observation first, then a plain-English translation of what it means for customers and sales.

Catalog Architecture All 115 products live on a single scrolling page
The /products/ page renders the entire catalog at once. No pagination, no category landing pages, no filters, no search. Products are organized into three hand-curated sections (Formulas, Singles, Hydrosols) that all live on the same URL.
What this means When someone arrives looking for a sleep formula, whether from Google, Instagram, or a friend's recommendation, they land on a wall of 115 products and have to scroll. There's nothing for them to click. Most leave before they find what they came for. This is the single biggest reason your traffic isn't converting the way it could.
Catalog Architecture The same products appear in multiple sections
On the listing page, oils like Frankincense, Lavender, Rose Otto, and Tea Tree appear under both a featured group and the full singles list. This isn't a bug, it's a tell that the page was hand-curated rather than driven by your product database.
What this means This is exactly the reason adding a new product is so painful. The page is built like a magazine layout instead of a catalog. Every new product means editing the layout, not just adding the product. Once the rebuild is in place, "add a product" really does mean just adding the product.
Product Template Pop-up content is showing on the page itself
On product pages (e.g. Acne Solution), content meant to appear inside the "Practical Applications" pop-up, compress instructions, patch testing guidelines, the olive oil safety protocol, is rendering directly on the page. In some cases it appears two or three times in a row.
What this means The product page looks broken. Instructions that should only appear when a customer clicks an icon are showing up multiple times in the middle of the product description. This is happening on every product because they all share the same broken template. Right now, every visitor to your highest-traffic pages sees a page that looks like it has bugs.
Product Template Application icons render as plain letters
The "Practical Applications" section shows C, S, M, N in place of icons for Compress, Skincare, Massage, and Neat.
What this means Where customers should see beautiful icons hinting at how to use the oil, they see what looks like a typo: four single letters. There's no visual cue that those letters are meant to be tapped. Most customers don't.
Product Template A product's address reveals the copy-paste problem
The Facial Rejuvenation Kit lives at the URL /products/lavender-bundle-copy/. That slug is a leftover from when someone duplicated a different product page to set this one up.
What this means The address bar tells the story of how the site has been maintained: by copying existing pages and editing them. This kind of artifact accumulates over time, and it makes the site harder to maintain every year. The rebuild eliminates the need for this kind of workaround entirely.
Performance The site loads a key piece of software twice
The page loads jquery.min.js once from WordPress core and again from the theme's bundled copy in node_modules. Two separate downloads of the same library on every page view.
What this means Every visitor downloads the same script twice, like pouring two cups of coffee when you only need one. It slows the site down for no benefit. Google's algorithm penalizes slow sites in search rankings. Mobile shoppers on weaker connections feel it the most.
Performance The homepage loads 20 separate scripts
Including duplicate jQuery, plus Foundation, What-Input, WooCommerce frontend bundle, Google Tag Manager, WordPress.com Stats, Jetpack analytics, sourcebuster, and several theme scripts. The SG Optimizer plugin is installed but not configured to combine these.
What this means Each one of those 20 scripts is a separate trip the browser has to make before your page becomes usable. You're already paying for a plugin that's supposed to combine them, it's just never been configured. This is fixable in a few hours and the page will feel noticeably faster.
Performance Each page carries its own custom styling
The homepage alone contains 12 inline <style> blocks totaling roughly 12 KB of custom CSS. These are mostly Elementor-generated overrides specific to that single page. None of it is shared or cached across the site.
What this means Every page on your site is carrying its own bundle of style overrides, none of it shared between pages, none of it cached. Each page reload starts the styling work from scratch. It also means small visual tweaks tend to break things, because the styling is fragmented across hundreds of places instead of one.
Performance Phone shoppers are downloading desktop-sized images
Of 129 images on the homepage: 109 PNG, 5 JPEG, 8 SVG, 2 WebP. None use the srcset attribute, which means there's no mechanism to serve a smaller image to a smaller screen.
What this means A customer on an iPhone is downloading the same full-resolution product photos as a customer on a 27-inch desktop monitor, even though the phone screen is a quarter of the size. Phone shoppers are now the majority of online traffic. Right now, they're working harder than they need to just to see your products.
Accessibility Three out of four product photos have no description
98 of 129 <img> tags on the homepage have empty alt attributes. Alt text is the short description attached to an image that screen readers announce and search engines read.
What this means Two consequences. First: when a visually-impaired shopper visits using a screen reader, every product image is announced as "image", they hear silence instead of "Acne Solution roll-on, 5ml." Second: Google can't see what's in your images, so you don't show up in image searches like "lavender essential oil bottle." Adding alt text is a small task that opens up two whole channels.
SEO Your Google listing ends with a stray % character
The current homepage title tag reads: "Organic Essential Oil & French Medical Aromatherapy | Melissa's Botanicals %", the trailing % is a Yoast SEO template token (%%page%% or similar) that didn't resolve.
What this means Every time someone Googles your business, that's what they see in the search results. A stray percent sign at the end of your name. It looks broken, and search results are where most first impressions happen now. Five-minute fix; been there for a while.
SEO Search engines can't see your products as products
Product pages don't emit structured data for Product, Offer, or AggregateRating. This is the markup that lets Google show rich product cards in search results.
What this means When a competitor product appears in Google search, you see the star rating, the price, and an "in stock" badge right in the result. Your products appear as plain blue links. The visual difference is enormous and the click-through rates are dramatically different. The fix is invisible markup that tells Google "this is a product, here's its price, here's its rating." It's standard work that's never been done.
Owner Control Adding a product requires a developer, not you
The current architecture treats every product detail page as an individually-built Elementor layout rather than a single template that all products inherit from. There's no shared template, so every "new product" means hand-building a new page.
What this means This is the root reason every change runs through a developer. It's also the thing we fix first. Once the catalog is on a unified template, adding a product really is just: name, description, photo, price, click save. You can do it from your phone if you want.
The work, by area

Scope of work

What we're proposing is a brand-new website, not a tune-up of the existing one. The current site stays online during the build; the new site goes live as a complete replacement. The scope below covers six work areas in that build. Pricing scales with the growth services you choose to layer in, covered in the pre-proposal.

01 · Catalog Architecture
Make your store browsable
So shoppers can find what they came for in seconds.
  • Real category pages, Formulas, Singles, Hydrosols, Skincare
  • Sub-categories within Formulas: Pain, Skin, Wellness, Facial, Deodorant
  • Live search bar with type-ahead suggestions
  • Filter pills, by use case, by category, by price
  • Sort options, by name, price, popularity
  • Pagination (or load-more on mobile)
02 · Product Template
One clean page that all 115 products share
So every product looks polished and the modal bleed-through goes away.
  • Single unified layout that every product inherits from
  • Fix pop-up bleed-through (compress, patch test, safety protocol)
  • Replace letter placeholders with real icons
  • Clean up size variations and pricing display
  • Related products / cross-sells driven by category
  • Consistent image gallery with zoom and swipe
03 · Image Pipeline
Phones load fast, every photo is described
So your site feels fast on phones and shows up in image searches.
  • Convert product imagery to WebP/AVIF (modern formats) with fallback
  • Generate responsive size variants for phone, tablet, desktop
  • Write descriptive alt text for every product image
  • Standardize image dimensions across the catalog
04 · SEO & Structured Data
Make Google see your products as products
So your Google listings show stars, prices, and "in stock" badges instead of plain links.
  • Fix the broken page title template (the stray %)
  • Add Product, Offer, and AggregateRating schema to every product
  • Per-product meta descriptions written for search
  • Sitemap audit and Google Search Console reconnect
  • Internal linking between related products and conditions
05 · Performance & Code Cleanup
Strip out the cruft that's slowing things down
So pages load faster and the site feels professionally maintained.
  • Remove the duplicate jQuery load
  • Audit the plugin list, retire what's unused
  • Consolidate inline page-specific CSS into the theme
  • Configure SG Optimizer's combine/minify (already installed, unused)
  • Remove per-page CSS overrides and !important patches
06 · Admin Handoff
You run the catalog from now on
So you can add, edit, or remove products yourself, no developer call required.
  • Full owner-managed product CRUD from the WooCommerce dashboard
  • 30-minute training session (live or recorded, your choice)
  • Written cheat-sheet for common tasks (add product, change price, swap photo)
  • 30 days of post-launch support for questions or fixes
