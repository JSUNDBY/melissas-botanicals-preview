What's running

The current foundation, in one paragraph.

The current site is built on WordPress with WooCommerce, with a page builder (Elementor) layered on top and several plugins adding behavior. The store engine itself is sound. The challenge is everything that's been bolted on around it over the years.

The platformWordPress · WooCommerce (the store engine)

ThemeCustom melissasbotanicals theme

What this means in plain language: your store engine is sound. The friction comes from the layers on top of it, the page-builder layouts that need a developer to touch, the plugin stack adding weight to every page, the custom CSS overrides patched in over years. The rebuild removes the bolt-ons and lets a modern commerce stack do its job.

Observations

Thirteen things worth fixing.

Each finding has the technical observation first, then the plain-English translation of what it means for customers and sales. Tagged by the area of work it lives in.

Catalog Architecture All 115 products on a single scrolling page
The /products/ page renders the entire catalog at once. No pagination, no category landing pages, no filters, no search. Products are organized into three hand-curated sections (Formulas, Singles, Hydrosols) that all share the same URL.
What this means When someone arrives looking for a sleep formula, whether from Google, Instagram, or a friend, they land on a wall of 115 products and have to scroll. Most leave before they find what they came for. This is the single biggest reason your traffic isn't converting the way it could.
Catalog Architecture The same products appear in multiple sections
On the listing page, oils like Frankincense, Lavender, Rose Otto, and Tea Tree appear under both a featured group and the full singles list. Not a bug, but a tell that the page was hand-curated rather than driven by your product database.
What this means This is exactly why adding a new product is so painful. The page is built like a magazine layout instead of a catalog. Once the rebuild is in place, "add a product" really does mean just adding the product.
Product Template Pop-up content is showing on the page itself
On product pages (e.g. Acne Solution), content meant to live inside the "Practical Applications" pop-up — compress instructions, patch testing guidelines, the olive oil safety protocol — is rendering directly on the page. In some cases it appears two or three times in a row.
What this means The product page looks broken. Instructions that should only appear when a customer clicks an icon are showing up multiple times in the middle of the product description. This is happening on every product because they all share the same broken template.
Product Template Application icons render as plain letters
The "Practical Applications" section shows C, S, M, N in place of icons for Compress, Skincare, Massage, and Neat.
What this means Where customers should see beautiful icons hinting at how to use the oil, they see four single letters. There's no visual cue that those letters are meant to be tapped. Most customers don't.
Product Template A product's web address reveals the copy-paste problem
The Facial Rejuvenation Kit lives at the URL /products/lavender-bundle-copy/. That slug is a leftover from when someone duplicated a different product page to set this one up.
What this means The address bar tells the story of how the site has been maintained: by copying existing pages and editing them. This kind of artifact accumulates over time. The rebuild eliminates the need for this kind of workaround.
Performance The site loads a key piece of software twice
The page loads jquery.min.js once from WordPress core and again from the theme's bundled copy. Two separate downloads of the same library on every page view.
What this means Every visitor downloads the same script twice. Like pouring two cups of coffee when you only need one. It slows the site down for no benefit. Search engines penalize slow sites in rankings. Mobile shoppers on weaker connections feel it the most.
Performance The homepage loads twenty separate scripts
Including duplicate jQuery, plus Foundation, What-Input, WooCommerce frontend bundle, Google Tag Manager, WordPress.com Stats, Jetpack analytics, sourcebuster, and several theme scripts. The SiteGround Optimizer plugin is installed but not configured to combine them.
What this means Each script is a separate trip the browser makes before your page becomes usable. You're already paying for a plugin that's supposed to combine them, it's just never been configured.
Performance Each page carries its own custom styling
The homepage alone contains 12 inline <style> blocks totaling roughly 12 KB of custom CSS. Mostly Elementor-generated overrides specific to that single page. None of it is shared or cached across the site.
What this means Every page on your site is carrying its own bundle of style overrides. None of it shared between pages, none of it cached. Each page reload starts the styling work from scratch. It also means small visual tweaks tend to break things, because the styling is fragmented across hundreds of places instead of one.
Performance Phone shoppers download desktop-sized images
Of 129 images on the homepage: 109 PNG, 5 JPEG, 8 SVG, 2 WebP. None use the srcset attribute, which means there's no mechanism to serve a smaller image to a smaller screen.
What this means A customer on an iPhone downloads the same full-resolution product photos as a customer on a 27-inch desktop, even though the phone screen is a quarter of the size. Phone shoppers are now the majority of online traffic. Right now they're working harder than they need to just to see your products.
Accessibility Three out of four product photos have no description
98 of 129 <img> tags on the homepage have empty alt attributes. Alt text is the short description attached to an image that screen readers announce and search engines read.
What this means Two consequences. First, a visually-impaired shopper using a screen reader hears "image" instead of "Acne Solution roll-on, 5ml." Second, search engines can't see what's in your images, so you don't show up in image searches like "lavender essential oil bottle." Adding alt text opens up two channels at once.
SEO Your search listing ends with a stray % character
The homepage title tag reads: "Organic Essential Oil & French Medical Aromatherapy | Melissa's Botanicals %". The trailing % is a Yoast SEO template token that didn't resolve.
What this means Every time someone Googles your business, that's what they see. A stray percent sign at the end of your name. It looks broken, and search results are where most first impressions happen now. A five-minute fix that's been there for a while.
SEO Search engines can't see your products as products
Product pages don't emit structured data for Product, Offer, or AggregateRating. This is the markup that lets search engines show rich product cards with stars, prices, and stock badges.
What this means When a competitor product appears in a search result, you see the star rating, the price, and an "in stock" badge right in the listing. Your products appear as plain blue links. The visual difference is enormous and the click-through rates are dramatically different. The fix is invisible markup that tells search engines "this is a product, here's its price, here's its rating."
Owner Control Adding a product requires a developer, not you
The current architecture treats every product detail page as an individually-built Elementor layout rather than a single template that all products inherit from. There's no shared template.
What this means This is the root reason every change runs through a developer. It's also the thing we fix first. Once the catalog is on a unified template, adding a product really is just: name, description, photo, price, click save. You can do it from your phone if you want.
The work, by area

Six areas of work, each one mapping back to what we found.

Pricing scales with which areas you include and which layered services you add. We align on the final number after walking through this together. The pre-proposal covers the layered services in detail.

i.
Catalog Architecture
Make your store browsable.
  • Real category pages: Formulas, Singles, Hydrosols, Skincare
  • Sub-categories within Formulas: Pain, Skin, Wellness, Facial, Deodorant
  • Live search with type-ahead suggestions
  • Filter pills by use case, category, and price
  • Sort options by name, price, popularity
  • Pagination or load-more on mobile
ii.
Product Template
One clean page that all 115 products share.
  • Single unified layout every product inherits from
  • Fix pop-up bleed-through (compress, patch test, safety protocol)
  • Replace letter placeholders with real icons
  • Clean up size variations and pricing display
  • Related products and cross-sells driven by category
  • Consistent image gallery with zoom and swipe
iii.
Image Pipeline
Phones load fast, every photo is described.
  • Convert product imagery to modern formats (WebP, AVIF) with fallback
  • Generate responsive size variants for phone, tablet, desktop
  • Write descriptive alt text for every product image
  • Standardize image dimensions across the catalog
iv.
SEO & Structured Data
Help search engines see your products as products.
  • Fix the broken page title template (the stray %)
  • Add Product, Offer, and AggregateRating schema to every product
  • Per-product meta descriptions written for search
  • Sitemap audit and Google Search Console reconnect
  • Internal linking between related products and conditions
v.
Performance & Code Cleanup
Strip out the bolt-ons that slow everything down.
  • Remove the duplicate jQuery load
  • Audit the plugin list, retire what's unused
  • Consolidate inline page-specific CSS into the theme
  • Configure SiteGround Optimizer's combine and minify (already installed)
  • Remove per-page CSS overrides and !important patches
vi.
Admin Handoff
You run the catalog from now on.
  • Full owner-managed product CRUD from the new admin
  • 30-minute training session (live or recorded)
  • Written cheat-sheet for common tasks (add product, change price, swap photo)
  • 30 days of post-launch support for questions or fixes
