How the work is organized

One new site. Layered services around it.

The new website is the core build. A complete new customer-facing site on modern commerce infrastructure, with an admin that's actually pleasant to use.

Layered services sit on top, modular and chosen with intent: video, programmatic content, email automation, founder-led publishing. We've grouped them by when to add them, so the conversation about scope is about when rather than whether.

Platform additions are larger optional capabilities, like a wholesale portal, subscription products, or international expansion. These get scoped separately when their time comes.

One commitment, one launch. Whatever is in for this round gets built, tested, and launched together.

The foundation

A faster, more stable commerce home, designed for long-term growth.

The new site lives on a modern commerce stack. Customers see a website that loads instantly from anywhere in the world. You log into an admin built specifically for running a store, not a blog platform with ecommerce bolted on.

The technical details are Cloudflare at the edge serving the customer-facing site, and Medusa (a modern open-source store engine) running products, orders, customers, and inventory behind it. You don't have to think about either name to use the site. The result is what matters: a calmer admin, a faster store, no plugin treadmill, and a foundation built for the next decade rather than the last one.

For your customers
A site that loads in milliseconds.
Served from a global edge network. Customers anywhere see your products load near-instantly. The current 100+ product scroll page becomes guided discovery with search, filters, and condition-based pathways.
For you
An admin built for ecommerce.
Logging in goes from "open WordPress, find the menu, click through Elementor, hope nothing breaks" to a clean modern interface designed for stores. Add a product in under two minutes. See orders at a glance. Update inventory with a click.
Included infrastructure
Modern perks, no monthly cost.
A global delivery network, free SSL, DDoS protection, automatic image optimization, and video hosting are all included on the platform's free tier. The store engine itself runs for under $20 per month.

The transition

How we move you off WordPress, safely.

Migration is the part of a rebuild that should never be rushed. Four steps, with your current site running the whole time, your sign-off required at every gate.

i.
Your current site keeps running.
Throughout the build, melissasbotanicals.com stays exactly where it is. Customers shop normally, orders flow normally. Nothing on the live site is touched.
ii.
Data migrates to staging.
Products, customer accounts, and order history are exported and brought into the new system on a private staging URL. Only you and Dawn can see it.
iii.
You test it before launch.
You spend as much time as you need on staging. Add test products, process test orders, explore the admin. Launch waits on your sign-off, every time.
iv.
Launch with the old site as backup.
Traffic moves to the new site. The old WordPress install stays online for 90 days as fallback. After 90 clean days, we archive it with a complete export.

A note on pricing

Scope first, then a single number.

Scope-dependent, by design.

The price follows the scope, not the other way around.

The foundation rebuild is one package. The layered services and platform additions are each priced individually. A focused engagement covering only the foundation and a couple of growth services looks very different from a comprehensive one layering in video, content, subscriptions, and a wholesale portal.

Rather than guess at scope before talking with you, we wait until the in-person visit, settle the scope together, and then quote a single fixed number for the engagement. No hourly billing. No surprise charges. Half on signature, half at launch.

The next step is the visit. The number follows from there, in writing, within forty-eight hours.
