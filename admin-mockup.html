The difference

A typical Tuesday morning

Three things you'd do on a normal day, today versus the new admin.

Today

Add a new product

Log into WordPress. Click Products. Click Add New. Fill in fields. Switch to Elementor to build the page layout. Hope you remember which template that other product used. Copy elements from an existing page. Paste. Adjust CSS. Preview. Find that something is broken because of a plugin conflict. Fix. Save. Pray.

Total time: 30-60 minutes, and it usually requires a developer.

New admin

Add a new product

Open the admin. Click + Add product. Fill in name, description, category, price, photo. Click Publish.

Total time: about two minutes. Done from your phone if you want.

Today

Change a product price

Log into WordPress. Find the product. Edit. Find which Elementor block has the price. Edit the block. Hope you got the right one. Save. Clear cache. Check the front-end. Cache didn't clear. Wait. Refresh.

New admin

Change a product price

Click the product. Edit price. Click Save. It's live on the site within seconds.

Today

Check what's low on stock

You don't, really. WooCommerce has inventory but the report is buried, and you find out you're out of something when a customer emails to ask.

New admin

Check what's low on stock

The dashboard shows you on login. You also get an email when something crosses your threshold. You catch low stock before customers do.

This is the admin you'll actually use

The current site has a powerful admin you avoid. The new one is built specifically for your business, clean, fast, and pleasant. You'll see this for the first time on staging before launch, so there's no surprise about what you're getting.
